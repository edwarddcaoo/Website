Real-Time Parking Availability Camera

This project was the final deliverable for my ENSC 100W course. Within a team of 8, our goal was to develop a program that would tell us if a parking spot was available, thus reducing the time staff and students would spend searching for one. I focused on the research part, in which I analyzed and identified the best possible equipment that would be able to withstand even the toughest weather conditions.

EWB UBC Biomedical Engineering Case Competition

This device was created to accomodate those with muscular dystrophy to participate in a variety of sports. In a team of 5, we all contributed to the design and development of the prototype, ensuring that it would meet all necessary criteria. Examples of criteria were: a price under $100, able to accommodate different ball sizes, and be easily transportable and storable without hindering the user's mobility.

Sumo Bot

This was an individual project that would later be used to compete against other students in my Robotics class. The goal was to create a robot that could not only defend itself from attacks, but also be able to knock out its opponents. The wooden controller was programmed to whatever was desired, in my case, I included mechanisms that would only activate when all four levers were pressed.
